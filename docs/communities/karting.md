# Karting — community brief

*US grassroots karting. Verified against 2025–2026 sources. Dirt-oval sanctioning map is the softest area (regionally diffuse).*

## The single most important thing
US karting is **not one sport**. The biggest fault line is **sprint / road-race karting vs. dirt-oval karting** — different tracks, chassis, engines, culture, vocabulary, and largely different people who rarely cross over. **A platform that treats "karting" as one generic category feels wrong to BOTH groups at once.** This divide should drive product + copy more than anything else. → **Let users pick their discipline** and adapt vocabulary.

## Sanctioning & structure (fragmented — no single governing body)
- **National sprint/road-race (high-dollar):** **SKUSA** (Pro Tour; X30/shifter), **USPKS**, **ROK Cup USA** (issued a joint "Statement of Intent" to align — underscores how separate they normally are).
- **Broad-tent:** **WKA** (largest in N. America; runs ~5 national series across sprint, **Speedway Pavement**, **Speedway Dirt**, Road Racing, **Gold Cup**). *Nuance:* the **Gold Cup is sprint-style road-course racing on Briggs 4-cycle** (Charlotte, G&J, New Castle, Pitt Race); the **Speedway Dirt Series** is the actual dirt-oval program (separate WKA Dirt Rulebook). Don't conflate Gold Cup with dirt. Also **AKRA** (asphalt + dirt), **IKF** (original, West-Coast), **KART**.
- **Dirt-oval world:** separate ecosystem (eastern/southern US + Midwest, 1/8–1/4-mile clay), dominated by **regional sanctions + local club series** more than national tours (medium confidence).
- **Real volume = club racing** at individual tracks (OVKA, PKRA, North Texas Karters, etc.).

## Classes & progression
Defined on **two axes: engine package × age/weight.**
- **Engine (cheapest→priciest):** **Briggs LO206** (4-stroke, **factory-sealed** spec, ~9hp, ~$550 engine / <$4.5k turnkey — **the heart of grassroots**; "if the rulebook doesn't say you can, you can't") → **KA100** (2-stroke step-up) → **X30 / Rotax** (premium TaG 125cc single-speed) → **Shifter (KZ)** (125cc 6-speed, top tier, 16+).
- **Age (varies):** Kid Kart (~5–7), Cadet (~7–12), Junior (~12–15), Senior (16+), Masters (~30/35+). Most clubs run separate LO206 Junior/Senior/Masters.
- **Progression — be honest:** **no single national ladder** (unlike BMX/MX). Movement is fragmented + self-directed across three independent axes: engine (LO206→KA100→X30/Rotax→shifter), age class, and competition level (club→regional→national series). Plenty of adults race LO206 at club level forever — a respected choice, not a failure. **Do not imply a linear ranked ladder.**

## Race-day format
- **Sprint:** practice → **qualifying** (timed) → **heats** → **prefinal** (declining) → **final**. Run in groups.
- **Dirt oval:** **hot laps → heats → (B-main/consi) → A-main feature**, field inversions common; "pill draw," "the cushion."
- Concrete copy trap: a dirt racer doesn't run a "prefinal"; a sprint racer doesn't run a "feature."

## Racers & culture
- Ethos: "purest form of motorsport," "where champions start" (F1 pipeline: Hamilton/Alonso/Verstappen/Vettel). Driver exposed, "no hiding."
- Overwhelmingly a **family sport**; parents are crew/mechanics/budget; kids start ~5, adults race for life.
- **Two poles in sprint:** the **LO206 spec community** (cost-conscious, egalitarian, skill-over-spending, the grassroots soul — wary of being treated as second-class) vs **high-dollar national** (X30/shifter, tents/tuners/travel). **Dirt-oval culture** is its own thing (closer to dirt-track stock-car/sprint-car, working-class, regional pride).
- Shared values: cost-consciousness, family, mechanical self-reliance, suspicion of marketing fluff and outsiders.

## Pain points / software opportunities
- **Fragmented schedules** (multiple sanctions + home club, across sites/Facebook) — the #1 connect-the-dots gap.
- Regional/club **points tracking** (lives on club sites/PDFs).
- **Setup logging by track + weather** (gearing, RPM/lap, tire, **jetting** — weather-critical for 2-strokes). A dedicated app (Kart Track App w/ weather + AI) already exists → must do this **credibly or not at all**.
- **Budget tracking** (tires/fuel/oil/chains/engine) — emotionally central, esp. LO206.
- **Newcomer class confusion** ("which class for my kid's age + budget"); arrive-and-drive vs ownership decision; used-kart buying is the normal entry path.

## Where they live online
Forums: **KartPulse** (central modern hub, mission = the sport's fragmentation), eKartingNews, **Bob's 4 Cycle / 4cycle.com** (LO206/setup), Reddit r/gokarts. Timing/results: **MyLaps Speedhive** (TR2, Orbits standings), **Race Monitor**. Registration: **MotorsportReg** (common, not universal) + club sites + Facebook. Sanction sites: worldkarting.com, superkartsusa.com, uspks.com, akraracing.com.

## How to speak to them — and what we likely get WRONG
**Signals you get it:**
- **Lead with LO206** as a first-class citizen (not a footnote) — the single biggest grassroots tell. "Level playing field," "sealed-engine racing," "the driver makes the difference."
- Respect cost-consciousness ("built for racing families," consumables tracking, used-kart friendly); avoid luxury/lifestyle framing.
- Right race-day nouns per discipline; honor F1 lineage without cringe; family/crew framing.
- **Dual-audience rule:** let users pick **sprint vs dirt-oval** and adapt vocab. Sprint: qualifying→heats→prefinal→**final**, grid, X30/shifter/Rotax/KA100/LO206. Dirt: hot laps→heats→**feature/main**, pill draw, the cushion, clay. Don't rank one above the other.

**What a generic platform gets wrong:**
1. Treating karting as one homogeneous category (collapsing sprint + dirt).
2. Burying/ignoring **LO206**.
3. Implying a linear national **ladder** (false for karting).
4. Generic race-day labels matching neither world; applying "prefinal/grid" universally.
5. Ignoring **weather-linked setup logging** (jetting/gearing) — absence reads as "they don't actually race."
6. Supporting only one sanction's calendar (families juggle multiple + a home club).
