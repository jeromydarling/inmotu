# Grassroots / sportsman drag racing — community brief

*US. Verified against 2025–2026 NHRA/IHRA/track sources. Dated specifics need annual re-check.*

## Sanctioning & structure
- **NHRA** dominant, a pyramid: **Pro/National** (Top Fuel/Funny Car/Pro Stock — NOT our audience) → **7 geographic Divisions** (1 Northeast … 7 West), each with its own points + calendar → **Lucas Oil Drag Racing Series (LODRS)**, the sportsman/divisional tour → **~130 NHRA member tracks** running weekly programs.
- **IHRA** — second-largest (~117 tracks), now grassroots/bracket-focused after acquiring **WDRA** (2025); flagship = **Summit SuperSeries**.
- **Unsanctioned/independent tracks** run their own bracket + test-and-tune programs. "Run what ya brung."
- **Key insight:** grassroots drag is radically decentralized and **local-track-centric**; bracket racing lets anyone be competitive in almost any vehicle.

## Classes & progression
- **Bracket racing** (the heart): handicap racing on a predicted ET (**dial-in**); slower car gets a head start; run closest to your dial **without breaking out**. Two flavors: **footbrake/no-electronics** (purer, cheaper) vs **electronics/delay box** (transbrake + delay box, more consistent/expensive). Usually split so footbrakers aren't outgunned.
- Track bracket tiers: **Super Pro** (electronics), **Pro** (footbrake), **Sportsman** (street/slower), plus **Motorcycle**, **Jr. Dragster**.
- **Sportsman index classes** (LODRS): **Super Comp** (8.90), **Super Gas** (9.90), **Super Street** (10.90); **Stock/Super Stock** (classified by weight ÷ factored HP into 50+ sub-classes).
- **The ladder (emotional core):** track championship (weekly points) → **NHRA Summit Racing Series** (national bracket program, ~80k racers; top track points-finishers go as a **team**) → **Division ET Finals** (champion's jacket + purse) → **Summit Racing Series World Championship**, Las Vegas. IHRA parallel: track → **Summit SuperSeries World Finals**. The "make the jacket / go to Vegas" dream is attainable for a working family with one car.

## Race-day format (typical Saturday)
Gates/**tech** → **time trials/qualifying** (or a pure **test-and-tune** night, ~$20–30) → **set your dial-in** (from ETs + weather) → **eliminations** (single-elim **ladder**). **The Tree:** sportsman/full tree (ambers 0.5s apart) vs **pro tree** (all ambers, green 0.4s later); modern perfect light = **0.000**. Win by combining **reaction time** + closest to dial **without breakout**; **red light** = auto loss. Tracks **1/8** or **1/4 mile** (grassroots heavily eighth-mile).

## Racers & culture
- **Jr. Dragsters:** ages **5–17**, half-scale dragsters (~5 hp, ~85 mph/7.90 in the eighth), classes by age + ET cap; entry car ~$5k; NHRA membership required; earns track points. The on-ramp that builds lifelong, multi-generational racers.
- **Adults:** overwhelmingly working/middle-class; bracket racing is deliberately budget-accessible. Multi-generation teams the norm (grandfather/parent/kid, one trailer).
- Values: family, self-reliance ("run what ya brung"), consistency over horsepower, the home track, frugality, the **time slip** as the receipt of a hard-earned run. **NOT** the road-course/track-day crowd.

## Pain points / software opportunities
- **Event discovery is broken** — no central calendar; racers hunt track sites/Facebook/word-of-mouth for test-and-tune nights, points races, rain-outs. **The #1 unmet need.**
- **Track points tracking** opaque (Facebook posts/PDFs); no clear view of season position or path to ET Finals.
- **Weather / air density (DA)** for tuning/dial prediction (today: RaceAir, Air Density Online, DragTuner) — high value to bundle.
- **Time slip / ET history logging** (most keep paper or notebooks); existing apps (Crew Chief Pro, DragTuner) clunky/hardcore, not family-friendly.
- Jr. Dragster scheduling + multi-driver/one-trailer logistics; divisional points-chase planning; acute budget consciousness.

## Where they live online
Discovery: individual **track sites + Facebook pages/groups** (primary for rain-outs), NHRA track locator. Results/timing: track-specific, some RaceHero/Race Monitor, DragRaceCentral/nhraracer.com. Weather/DA: Air Density Online, DragTuner, RaceAir hardware. Tuning logbook: Crew Chief Pro. Fragmented — **no one owns the family-friendly all-in-one** "find races + track points + log slips + weather."

## How to speak to them
- Reference the **time slip**, **the tree**, **dial-in**, **60-foot**, **breakout**, **the green**, the **home track**, the **eighth-mile**.
- Honor family/multigeneration ("From the Jr. Dragster to Grandpa's footbrake car — one trailer, one app."), the **ladder dream** ("Track points → Division ET Finals → Vegas."), budget ("You don't need a $200k car to win Saturday night."), **DA-aware tuning**.
- Phrases: "Closest to the dial wins." / "Cut a perfect light." / "From Jr. Dragster to the World Finals."
- **Avoid:** lap times, apexes, road courses, track days, supercars, lap-record framing, generic "motorsport" gloss — instantly marks the app as outsider.

## Confidence
Jr. Dragster ET caps vary by season/track; IHRA track count (~117) and LODRS event counts (2025: 45 divisional + 21 regional) and Summit payouts ($6k/$2k/$500; $3k travel) are **dated — re-verify annually**.
