# BMX racing — community brief

*US BMX racing. Verified against 2025–2026 sources (USA BMX, Wikipedia, community/track sites).*

## Sanctioning & structure
- **USA BMX** is effectively the single national sanctioning body for BMX *racing*. Founded 1977 as the **ABA**; merged with rival **NBL** (founded 1974) in **June 2011** → rebranded **USA BMX**. ~300+ tracks (US, Canada, Puerto Rico), 70,000+ members. (USA Cycling governs Olympic/elite + freestyle — don't conflate.)
- **Parallel ranking layers** (important for product): **District** → **State** → **Gold Cup region** → **National** → **NAG (National Age Group)**.
- **Season:** weekly local racing (district/state points) → **Gold Cup Championship Series** (regions: Northwest, North Central, Northeast, Southeast, South Central; chase 1-2-3 plates) → **National Series** (national + NAG points) → **Grand Nationals ("the Grands")**, Tulsa OK, Thanksgiving week (the championship finale, #1 plates) → **Race of Champions (ROC)** alongside.

## The ladder / "road to nationals"
Two axes: **proficiency class** (skill) × **age group** (single year of age).
- **Proficiency:** **Novice → Intermediate → Expert → Pro.** Advance by wins: ~10 wins Novice→Int; ~20 wins Int→Expert (10 for girls). Earning a NAG/Gold Cup/ROC plate force-bumps to ≥ Intermediate.
- **Age groups:** single-year classes (boys ~6–16, girls ~6–14), consolidating into combined/**Vet** classes for adults. You race your proficiency *within* your age (e.g. "9 Intermediate").
- **Balance Bike / Strider** class: ~18 months–5 yrs, non-pointed, race "half track" — the on-ramp.
- **Cruiser:** 24" bikes, **age-only** (no proficiency); many "double-class" 20" + cruiser same day.
- **Points → rankings:** points at every race feed district/state/Gold Cup/national. **NAG** = national prestige; **top 10 earn NAG plates**; standings use **best 8 scores**. NAG #1–10 are removed from district rankings after the Grands.

## Race-day format
- **Moto** = a qualifying race (run 3); **max 8 riders**, **gates 1–8** (1 inside, 8 outside).
- **Moto sheet** = posted lineup (rider, gate, moto). **>8 riders** → motos → quarters → semis → **main** via **transfer**; ≤8 → 3 motos decide the **main**.
- **Gate pick** by finish order; **staging** marshals riders onto the gate.
- Typical local day: sign-ups 1–3 hrs before, practice, 3 motos, mains, awards (a few hours). Tracks: outdoor **clay/dirt** dominate; some indoor. Features: start hill/gate, berms, rhythm/jump sections, sometimes a pro-set.

## Families & culture
- All ages — balance-bike toddlers through 60s+ and pros; **whole family races the same day**. Multi-generational, lawn chairs, team tents, racer + pit crew + volunteers. Values: sportsmanship, respect, consistency, "earn your plate."
- Tone: energetic, rad, rock-'n'-roll, family-friendly, **proud of low barrier to entry** (bike + helmet).
- **vs. motocross:** culturally very similar (BMX = "bicycle motocross"; same gate/moto/main, plate culture). Differences: far cheaper, safer, starts much younger, human-powered, short sprints. Frame BMX as the **affordable, kid-accessible cousin of MX**.

## Pain points / software opportunities
- **Unified standings** across district/state/Gold Cup/NAG with "what you need next" projections (best-8 aware) — the clearest gap.
- Faster result confirmation (track uploads lag 2–3 days).
- Single filterable **calendar** (local + Gold Cup + National) with travel context.
- Registration is inconsistent (often in-person 1–3 hrs before; per-track third-party like Presign).
- Membership renewal + gate-fee reminders, budget visibility.
- Grands/ROC **qualification clarity**; **"wins to next class"** counter; multi-rider household management.

## Where they live online
usabmx.com (Find a Track, Events, View Points, moto sheets/results, rulebook, app, *Pull* magazine); per-track registration (Presign etc.); USA BMX track directory + points DB are canonical; FloBikes (streaming), BMX-Insider, forums, local Facebook groups.

## How to speak to them
- Their words: moto, main, gate pick, transfer, NAG plate, Gold Cup, the Grands, plate — **not** heat/final/rankings.
- "Track your road to the **#1 plate**." / "From your first **balance-bike moto** to the **Grands in Tulsa**." / "Know what it takes to **transfer to the main**."
- "One account, your **whole crew** — every rider, every class, every gate fee."
- Warm, gritty, family-first; values: sportsmanship, community, earning it, all-ages.
